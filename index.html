My Project

Bush Tucker App

For some time now I’ve been wishing someone would bring out a Native Bush Tucker app. It would encompass all aspects of ‘bush tucker’.

Contents

1. Traditional uses.

2. Growing conditions

2.2 Tolerances, frost, salinity etc

3. Plant description

3.1 Annual, perennial,

3.2 Height, width, prostrate, upright etc

4. Tips and advice from other users

5. Warnings eg Kangaroo apple is poisonous unless fully ripe.

6. Commercial success, e.g. macadamias

7. Retail section. Eg Links to businesses with Bushtucker products.

The Uses For The App/Overview

Say someone tries a bit of bush tucker while travelling. [I had a Quandong pie at Blinman in the Flinders Ranges, yum.] Maybe they did a bush tucker tour with fellow travellers at a national park somewhere. They love that food! They want to grow it at home. Where do they find it? Will it grow at their place? How do they prepare it once it grown? All this information and more would be available on the app. It would have a welcome page with a searchable index listing of all the bush tucker plants. Each plant would then have relevant information and any retailers with that plant or a product produced from it could link their online listing to it.

Motivation

The above mentioned Quandong pie! I have been trying to grow Quandongs for a couple of years now. They are so tricky!

Collaboration

I would need to enlist the expertise of a number of collaborators that have knowledge in the field. The app would require input for the different climatic zones across the country.

Retailers of Native Bush Tucker plants could have a section where they can sell their wares.

Users would be able to upload data to the app.

Tools And Technology Required

Hardware

I would be using my old Mac. I was going to do an initial development for android but as I have been looking into app development more I have found there are app building platforms where you can develop apps for Android and IOS simultaneously. I am assuming that for the sake of the project the idea is to write the native code from scratch so I would write for Android.

Software.

Appypie Appmakr. Software may need a compiler.

Open source. Github

Outcome

The desired outcome would be that any user would have at their fingertips the ability to identify, procure and therefore use edible plants from across Australia. Whether they wished to actually grow the plants themselves, obtain any of the unique herbs, spices, fruits or vegetables, or even just learn about Bush Tucker the information would all be there in one place. Some states do have restrictions on the importation of plant matter but hopefully they would find a local stockist amongst the retail section.

I have recently started a small Edible Native Garden. Personally, I would find this app very useful and hopefully others would too.
